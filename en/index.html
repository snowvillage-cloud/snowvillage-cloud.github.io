What is SnowVillage?

SnowVillage is the Japanese user community around Snowflake. Conversation lives in Slack, where members organize themselves into interest-based sub-groups — we call them "user groups". Those user groups form around technology & functional topics, industries, and activity styles.

Each user group operates semi-autonomously and runs its own meetups, so somewhere in Japan, a SnowVillage-affiliated event happens almost every week. Beyond events, user groups also produce ongoing online content — YouTube streams, hands-on series, and community-run writing — so that knowledge keeps flowing even between meetups.

In other words, SnowVillage is less a single organization and more a network of engineer-led sub-communities connected by Slack, shared values, and a common platform.

Series

Frosty Friday Live Challenge

Operated by: #FrostyFriday user group

A live challenge series based on the globally run "Frosty Friday" Snowflake exercises. Community members tackle each week's problem on air and share their own take in real time.

Why we love it Guests raise their hand for the challenges that match their specialty, so each episode delivers a jaw-dropping solution you would never have thought of yourself. The regular hosts add friendly, technical commentary on top — so whether you are a beginner or an advanced user, you can treat it as entertainment and still come away smarter.

Series

25 Days of Streamlit

Operated by: #Streamlit user group

A 25-day advent-calendar-style hands-on series combining Streamlit and Snowflake. Build one small piece each day in December and end up with a working app on Christmas Day.

Why we love it "Tech blog Advent Calendars" have been a beloved tradition in Japanese tech communities for years — a different author writes each day from December 1st to the 25th, on a shared theme. This series leans fully into that spirit: by day 25 you've built a Streamlit + Snowflake app where Santa delivers Christmas cards. It's a playful, true-to-form advent calendar that also happens to be a great on-ramp for building analytics apps on Snowflake.

Who runs SnowVillage

SnowVillage is kept alive by three layers of volunteers: the Mayors who coordinate the whole community, the Neighbors who pitch in with events and skills, and the user group operators who actually run each sub-community.

Mayors

Community leads coordinating the whole ecosystem

Mayors drive SnowVillage across four areas — Event, Brand, Growth, and Operation. They act as the first point of contact for anything community-related and keep the overall direction aligned.

See Mayors profile (Japanese) →

Neighbors

Reliable neighbors growing the community with us

Neighbors are community supporters recruited annually. They help with specific events, bring their own skills, and keep the ecosystem warm and approachable — like the dependable neighbor next door.

See Neighbors page (Japanese) →

User Group Operators

Operators who run each sub-community

Each SnowVillage user group — organized by technical area, industry, or activity style — has its own operators. They plan and run meetups, produce online content, and steer the group's culture. Their energy is what makes "somewhere every week" possible.
